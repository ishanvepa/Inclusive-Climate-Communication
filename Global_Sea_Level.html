• This chart displays the change in global sea level from 1880 to 2023, compared to the 1993-2008 average.
  • Tide gauge data (blue) and satellite measurements (purple) both reflect an upward trend.

A steady rise is evident, with global sea levels increasing by approximately 101 mm (4 inches) from 1993 to 2022. Primarily due to melting glaciers and the thermal expansion of seawater as the planet warms.
